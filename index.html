Who We Are

A Studio of One.
Building Worlds.

PARKDEOMES is an independent game development studio founded by JJ Park, a product marketing leader who has contributed to global launches of multiple AAA titles including Civilization and NBA 2K.

The studio focuses on building original games through small, testable prototypes and iterative design. Rather than starting with large production pipelines, PARKDEOMES explores gameplay ideas through rapid experimentation, validating systems and player decisions before expanding development.

The current project, JJ Park's: Subscriber Zero, is a deckbuilding roguelike set inside Korean streaming culture, exploring narrative-driven strategy systems and player choice. Currently experimenting with AI-assisted workflows and rapid prototyping tools to accelerate early development.

PARKDEOMES is a small independent studio and may collaborate with external artists, composers, and developers as projects evolve.

Writing Philosophy

Devlog
Craft Notes

Each entry is written in the tradition of long-form narrative nonfiction - a specific scene before any thesis, human stories before data, puzzles left unresolved. The goal is to document the thinking, not just the output.

"A devlog isn't a changelog. It's the part of the process that never makes it into the game."

— JJ Park, PARKDEOMES

Cold Opens

Every entry opens with a scene, not a summary. A specific moment, a specific person, before any thesis is stated. The reader should not know what the entry is about until three paragraphs in.

Small to Big

One bug report becomes a theory about player psychology. One playtester's confusion reveals something universal about how humans navigate systems under pressure.

The Unresolved

Design problems that have not been solved are more interesting than solutions. Sharing the open question invites the reader into the work rather than positioning them as audience for the finished product.

Named People

Playtesters have names. They have specific reactions. "A player was confused by the energy system" is forgettable. "Sarah dropped the controller and said nothing for four seconds" is not.
